GETTING HERE

The museum is conveniently situated near Bergen Sentrum, making it easily accessible for visitors from various parts of the city. With its central location, you can easily reach the museum by public transportation, including buses, trams, and trains. Additionally, for those who prefer cycling, there are numerous bike racks available in the vicinity, offering a convenient and eco-friendly option for reaching the museum. Whether you choose to arrive by public transport or bike, getting to the museum is straightforward, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable visit for all patrons.

ADMISSION

The entrance is free for all. There are guided tours of the museum that leave every hour. These tours are 70 NOK per person and include a handy printed guide of the museum. If you would like to organise a guided tour for your group of 6 or more people, please contact us to arrange the tour

ACCESSIBILITY

At our museum, we prioritize accessibility and inclusivity to ensure that all visitors can fully enjoy their experience. That's why we've implemented various measures to accommodate individuals with different needs. For those with mobility challenges, we have wheelchair accessibility ramps strategically placed throughout the museum, providing easy access to all areas and exhibits. Additionally, we offer audio guides equipped with detailed descriptions of the displays, allowing visually impaired visitors to engage with the content in a meaningful way. Furthermore, our museum features braille display signs, enabling those with visual impairments to navigate the exhibits independently and learn about the fascinating artifacts on display. With these thoughtful accommodations in place, we strive to create an inclusive environment where everyone can explore, learn, and enjoy their museum visit to the fullest extent possible. discovery.
