GETTING HERE

The museum is conveniently located near Bergen Sentrum, making it easily accessible for visitors from all parts of the city. Thanks to its central location, reaching the museum is a breeze with various public transportation options available, including buses, trams, and trains that connect you seamlessly to our doorstep.

For those who prefer a more eco-friendly option, there are plenty of bike racks situated nearby, providing a secure spot to park your bicycle while you explore. Whether you’re traveling by public transport or cycling, getting to the museum is simple and hassle-free, ensuring a pleasant experience right from the moment you set out to visit.

We strive to make your journey as seamless as possible, allowing you to focus on exploring all the exciting exhibits and activities we have to offer. We look forward to welcoming you to our museum!

ADMISSION

The entrance to our museum is free for all, welcoming visitors from near and far to explore our fascinating exhibits.

For those looking to enhance their experience, we offer guided tours of the museum that depart every hour. These informative tours are priced at 70 NOK per person and include a handy printed guide of the museum, providing valuable insights into our exhibits and collections.

If you would like to organize a guided tour for your group of 6 or more people, please don’t hesitate to contact us. We are happy to assist you in arranging a personalized tour that caters to your group’s interests and needs, ensuring a memorable and enriching visit for everyone.

ACCESSIBILITY

At our museum, we prioritize accessibility and inclusivity to ensure that all visitors can fully enjoy their experience. We believe that everyone should have the opportunity to explore and engage with the wonders of science and history, which is why we’ve implemented various measures to accommodate individuals with different needs.

For those with mobility challenges, we have wheelchair-accessible ramps strategically placed throughout the museum, providing easy access to all areas and exhibits. These ramps are designed to ensure a smooth transition from one space to another, allowing everyone to enjoy the full range of our offerings without obstacles.

Additionally, we offer audio guides equipped with detailed descriptions of the displays, enabling visually impaired visitors to engage with the content in a meaningful way. These guides enhance the experience by providing context and storytelling that bring the exhibits to life.

Furthermore, our museum features braille display signs throughout the venue, allowing those with visual impairments to navigate the exhibits independently and learn about the fascinating artifacts on display. These signs are carefully placed to ensure that visitors can easily find their way and access important information about the exhibits.

With these thoughtful accommodations in place, we strive to create an inclusive environment where everyone can explore, learn, and enjoy their museum visit to the fullest extent possible. We are committed to making our museum a welcoming space for all, ensuring that every visitor leaves with a sense of wonder and inspiration.
